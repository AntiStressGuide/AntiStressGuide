Basis of the Calculation

The Enhanced Stress Index (ESI) is calculated from 10 categories, each rated on a 0–4 scale. These categories cover a combination of psychological, physical, and behavioral factors, such as perceived stress, energy levels, coping strategies, lifestyle habits, and physical activity. Participants select a value in each category according to their current condition.

To determine the overall stress level, the scores from all 10 categories are summed:

  • Minimum total score: 0 (all categories scored 0)
  • Maximum total score: 40 (all categories scored 4)

The total score is then divided into four levels, representing the overall stress risk:

  • Level 1: 0–10 → Low Stress / Low Risk
  • Level 2: 11–20 → Moderate Stress / Moderate Risk
  • Level 3: 21–30 → High Stress / High Risk
  • Level 4: 31–40 → Very High Stress / Very High Risk

This method provides a composite index reflecting overall stress while still allowing individualised affirmations for each category, as higher scores indicate greater concern in specific areas.
