Basis of the Calculation with Weightings

The Enhanced Stress Index (ESI) is calculated from 10 categories, each rated on a 0–4 scale. Each category is assigned a weight representing its influence on the overall stress score:

  • Perceived Stress: 20%
  • Energy & Vitality: 10%
  • Coping Ability / Resilience: 15%
  • Work-Life Balance: 10%
  • Sleep Quality: 10%
  • Nutrition: 10%
  • Physical Activity: 10%
  • Alcohol Use: 5%
  • Tobacco Use: 5%
  • Drug Use / Gambling: 5%

The weighted score for each category is calculated by multiplying the category score (0–4) by its weight. The overall stress score is the sum of all weighted scores:

  • Minimum total score: 0 (all categories scored 0)
  • Maximum total score: 4 (all categories scored 4) × 100% weighted sum = 4.0

The total weighted score is then divided into four levels representing overall stress risk:

  • Level 1: 0.0 – 1.0 → Low Stress / Low Risk
  • Level 2: 1.1 – 2.0 → Moderate Stress / Moderate Risk
  • Level 3: 2.1 – 3.0 → High Stress / High Risk
  • Level 4: 3.1 – 4.0 → Very High Stress / Very High Risk

This method provides a composite index reflecting overall stress while still allowing individualised affirmations for each category.
