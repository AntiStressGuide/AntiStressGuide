Basis of the Calculation

The Enhanced Stress Index is based on a combination of scientifically validated measures of stress, lifestyle habits, physical activity, and chronic health conditions. Its goal is to provide a composite risk score that reflects both psychological and physiological health factors.

  • 1. Stress & Well-Being (40%)
    • Based on self-reported Likert-scale questions (0–4) about perceived stress, energy levels, coping, and life satisfaction.
    • Reflects psychological stress load and averages multiple dimensions to reduce bias.
    • Weighted 40% because stress is a primary driver of health outcomes.
  • 2. Harmful Habits (20%)
    • Includes alcohol, tobacco, drug use, and gambling.
    • Each habit is scored from 0 (none) to 4 (very high risk).
    • High scores indicate behaviors that negatively impact health and increase stress vulnerability.
    • Weighted 20% to reflect lifestyle contribution to overall health risk.
  • 3. Chronic Health Conditions (20%)
    • Counts existing chronic diseases, medical conditions, or post-operative conditions.
    • Each condition contributes points to the score (max 4), representing physiological vulnerability.
    • Weighted 20%; chronic conditions increase susceptibility to stress and reduce resilience.
  • 4. Physical Activity (20%)
    • Scored inversely: more activity → lower score.
    • Improves stress resilience, cardiovascular health, and mental well-being.
    • Weighted 20% to emphasize protective effects.

The total score is calculated as:

Total Score = (Stress × 0.4) + (Habits × 0.2) + (Chronic × 0.2) + (Physical × 0.2)

Score ranges from 0 (Excellent / Low Risk) to 4 (High / Very High Risk).
